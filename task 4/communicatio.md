Poor digital communication can lead to serious misunderstandings. For example, a student once sent an email to a professor without a proper subject line, greeting, or polite tone while requesting important information. The message seemed rude and unclear, which resulted in the professor ignoring the email. This caused delays and confusion for the student.

This situation could have been avoided by following basic email etiquette, such as using a clear subject line, addressing the recipient respectfully, and maintaining a polite tone. Proper structure and clarity help convey the message effectively and create a positive impression. Digital communication reflects professionalism, and small mistakes can lead to big consequences. Therefore, it is important to communicate clearly, respectfully, and thoughtfully in all online interactions.
